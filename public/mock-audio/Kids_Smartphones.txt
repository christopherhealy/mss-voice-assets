Here is Chris' answer to the question "Should kids be given smartphones?"

Should kids be given smartphones? At what age? Great question, and I wish a lot of other parents were here. It's something that's in the public consciousness all the time. My opinion is straightforward: I think kids need to be given smartphones at a certain age, and I would say that age is around ten or eleven. The reason is that for safety and security, parents still think that in this modern world, kids do need access. It has to be supervised, but I think it's important.

Here is a counter argument:

Some people believe that children should not be given smartphones at a young age, such as ten or eleven. There are several reasons for this viewpoint. First, early exposure to smartphones can negatively affect children's mental health. Excessive screen time may lead to problems like anxiety, depression, and poor sleep habits.

Second, smartphones can distract children from their studies and reduce their attention span. When children spend too much time on their phones, they may struggle to focus on schoolwork and other important activities. This distraction can harm their academic performance.

Third, giving smartphones to children too early can expose them to inappropriate content and online dangers. Without mature judgment, children might encounter harmful websites, cyberbullying, or online predators. Even with supervision, it is difficult to control all risks.

In conclusion, delaying smartphone use until children are older can protect their mental health, improve focus on education, and reduce exposure to online risks. Therefore, it might be better to wait until children are more mature before giving them smartphones.
